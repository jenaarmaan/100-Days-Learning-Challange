# Day 3: The History of AI (Symbolic vs. Statistical vs. Deep Learning)

### 1) One-line definition (in your own words)
The history of AI describes how approaches evolved from rule-based symbolic reasoning to data-driven statistical methods and finally to deep learning using neural networks.

### 2) Problem it solves
**Why this exists**
- Helps understand why modern AI works the way it does.
- Shows limitations of past approaches.
- Guides better design decisions in current AI systems.

**What fails without it**
- Repeating past mistakes.
- Overhyping current methods.
- Lack of conceptual clarity about AI capabilities.

### 3) Core idea (intuition)
**Analogy**
AI evolution is like learning math:
- **Symbolic AI** → Memorizing formulas
- **Statistical AI** → Learning patterns from examples
- **Deep Learning** → Automatically discovering complex representations

**Evolutionary Path**
```
Symbolic AI (1950–1980)
   ↓ Rules, logic, expert systems
Statistical AI (1990–2010)
   ↓ Machine learning, probability
Deep Learning (2010–present)
   ↓ Neural networks, large data
```

### 4) How it works (high-level steps)
1. **Symbolic AI**: Encode human knowledge as rules and logic.
2. **Statistical AI**: Learn patterns from data using probability and ML algorithms.
3. **Deep Learning**: Use layered neural networks to automatically learn complex features from large datasets.

### 5) Strengths
- **Symbolic AI**
    - Explainable reasoning
    - Logical precision
- **Statistical AI**
    - Handles uncertainty
    - Learns from data
- **Deep Learning**
    - High accuracy on complex tasks
    - Automatic feature extraction

### 6) Weaknesses / failure cases
- **Symbolic AI**
    - Hard to scale rule creation
    - Brittle outside defined rules
- **Statistical AI**
    - Requires quality data
    - Feature engineering needed
- **Deep Learning**
    - Data-hungry
    - Often less interpretable

### 7) Where it is used in real systems
- **FAANG**: Recommendation engines, speech recognition, search ranking, computer vision systems.
- **Startups**: Chatbots, fraud detection, customer analytics, AI-powered SaaS products.

### 8) Keywords / terms to remember
- Symbolic AI
- Expert systems
- Machine learning
- Statistical inference
- Neural networks
- Deep learning
- Feature engineering
- Representation learning
